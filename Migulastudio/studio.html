The maker

Dariusz Migula

Dariusz began his training in Warsaw, learning the discipline of men’s tailoring before relocating to the Canary Islands. There, away from fashion capitals, he developed a quieter language — one that prioritises longevity over novelty, and touch over trend.

His approach is deliberately slow. Each piece is cut, sewn, and finished by his own hands or by a single assistant he has trained for years. The atelier produces no more than forty garments annually.

“I do not design collections. I respond to individuals.”

The place

Maspalomas, Gran Canaria

The atelier occupies a former pottery studio near the dunes — light‑filled, quiet, and isolated from retail districts. Clients are received by appointment only. Here, there are no racks of samples, no seasonal collections. Only a cutting table, a single fitting mirror, and shelves of natural fibres.

Dariusz believes that place shapes the garment. The Atlantic light, the dry air, the silence — all of it enters the cloth.

The principles

What defines the Migula hand

✂️

Single needle tailoring

Every visible seam is sewn by hand or on a vintage machine — no industrial overlock.

🧵

Natural fibres only

Wool, linen, cotton, silk. No synthetics, no blends, no compromise.

📏

Zero waste cutting

Patterns are nested to minimise remnant fabric; scraps are used for small accessories or returned to the mill.

“I am not interested in being remembered. I am interested in the garment being remembered — by the person who wears it.”
— Dariusz Migula
